# Education

## Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University

I graduated from the Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University, Department of Electronics and Electrical Communications Engineering, Class of 2025.

I graduated with an overall grade of **Very Good**, and my graduation project received an **Excellent** grade.

Studying at Cairo University's Faculty of Engineering was one of the most challenging and rewarding experiences of my life. The program demanded strong commitment, continuous learning, and the ability to handle multiple responsibilities simultaneously.

Throughout my five years of study, I worked on numerous assignments, laboratory sessions, technical reports, presentations, and engineering projects, often under very tight deadlines. This environment taught me how to manage my time effectively, prioritize tasks, and remain productive under pressure. One of the most valuable lessons I learned is that no matter how difficult or busy things become, every task has to be completed with responsibility and commitment.

Engineering also strengthened my curiosity and passion for science and technology. It inspired me to explore how software, electronics, mathematics, and communication systems work together to solve real-world problems. During my studies, I discovered my passion for backend software engineering, artificial intelligence, automation, and intelligent systems, while maintaining a strong appreciation for electronics, embedded systems, and wireless communication technologies.

The curriculum exposed me to a wide range of engineering disciplines, giving me both theoretical knowledge and practical experience in areas such as:

* Electronics
* Analog Communications
* Digital Communications
* Microwave Engineering
* Antenna Design
* Electromagnetic Waves
* Embedded Systems
* Microprocessors and Microcontrollers
* Computer Architecture
* Programming and Software Development
* Digital Signal Processing (DSP)
* Electrical Circuits
* Electronic Circuits
* Control Systems
* Networking Fundamentals
* Mathematics for Engineering
* Signals and Systems

One of the highlights of my academic journey was my graduation project, **Advanced Automotive Radar System Development**, sponsored by Brightskies.

The project involved designing and developing a complete automotive radar system operating in the 76–81 GHz frequency range using Texas Instruments AWR2944EVM hardware. It covered radar signal processing, antenna design, target detection, object tracking, and AI-based gesture recognition.

Working on this project significantly improved my knowledge of Digital Signal Processing (DSP), radar systems, machine learning, embedded systems, real-time software, and teamwork on large-scale engineering projects.

Looking back, my years at Cairo University shaped not only my technical skills but also my mindset. They taught me discipline, perseverance, structured problem-solving, continuous learning, and the importance of turning complex ideas into practical engineering solutions.
